Novel Contributions

The Pipeline

Six novel algorithmic contributions working together to move food from surplus to served — faster and fairer than any prior system.

01 — NC1
Temperature-Aware Perishability Decay
Real ambient temperature from Open-Meteo integrated into an exponential PI decay model. PI_final = min(1.0, PI_base + 0.15 × max(0, (temp−25)/15)).
Perishability Index
02 — NC2
Dynamic Per-NGO Demand Forecasting
Individualized Random Forest models trained for each of 500 NGO partners, capturing unique weekly seasonality and festival demand cycles. Avg R²=0.3643.
Demand Prediction
03 — NC3
Weather-Adjusted Expiry Risk Score
Heat index derived from real temperature and humidity is fused into allocation priority, ensuring high-risk food is moved first during India's summer months.
Risk Scoring
04 — NC4
Fairness-Aware PAPS Allocation
The Perishability-Aware Priority Score embeds a Gini monitoring penalty that reduces inequality from 0.725 (Random) to 0.600 — a 17.3% fairness improvement.
Gini Optimization
05 — NC5
Multi-Objective Pareto Scalarization
PAPS simultaneously minimizes waste, maximizes fairness, and optimizes logistics efficiency via weighted objective scalarization with sensitivity-validated weights.
Multi-Objective
06 — NC6
Cold-Chain Compatibility Routing
Storage-type-aware assignment ensures items with PI_final > 0.70 reach NGOs with refrigeration. Cold factor = 1.0 (cold) | 0.5 (dry) | 0.0 (none).
Cold Chain
